... I took the road less traveled by, And that has made all the difference.

Robert Frost

The Road Not Taken is the only poem I ever remember the name of, and has been one of the only poems I have ever known very well. I would like to think that I am a road less traveled kind of person. And because of that I am lucky to have experienced a lot of cool things that most of my peers have and probably will not have the chance or desire to. I was able to play Rugby in highschool, attend and graduate from a highly rated military college, serve six years in the United States Marine Corps, work as a contractor in Afghanistan, backpack through New Zealand, Thailand and Nepal among countless other pretty fun adventures.

Some of the more notable stories I have earned the privilege to tell often remind me of that poem and of how lucky I have been to experience those kinds of things. I learned how to fly helicopers in Afghanistan by Ukranians speaking broken english who usually spent their days stumbling along the broad line between too drunk and too hung-over. Our co-pilot did not speak good enough english to navigate the hot gun ranges we were often flying through so I needed to take the right seat. I had never worked in aviation before so on my off time I would sit in the control tower (HMMWV aka humvee) studying how to communicate properly in that context. Since I was in the right seat anyway and I was never too sure what side of the line the pilot was on, I decided it was a good time to ask for them to teach me how to fly the MI-8 MTV we were getting thrown around in all day and night. After about 2 weeks I dragging the nose through the valleys around FOB Shank pretty comfortably.
